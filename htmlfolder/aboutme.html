About Me

Rejuvenate Ganga is an NGO dedicated to revitalizing the Ganga River by promoting sustainable cleaning practices and spreading environmental awareness.

Founder: Aarav Singh

In an attempt to giving back to our own mother nature and inspired by my parents who hails from a tranquil and beautiful city in India located right on the bank of river Ganga, I decided on working towards restoration and preservation of river Ganga and its tributaries and launched the “Rejuvenate Ganaga” project.

During visits to my family, I have witnessed the profound significance of the Ganga, with hundreds of people gathering along its banks each day. Ganga river basin, which saw the rise of early civilization supports a vast population and diverse range of wildlife and is a source for drinking water, irrigation, livelihood and more across 11 states and serves an estimated 500 million people.

However, increasing pollution threatens their way of life and endangers the delicate ecosystems sustained by the river. Despite its iconic status and religious heritage, river Ganga today is facing formidable pollution pressures, accompanied by threats to its biodiversity and environmental sustainability.

I started my journey of restoration and rejuvenation of river Ganga by exploring and researching about Ganga basin in general, followed by understanding the key challenges and issues affecting the cleanliness and free-flow or river Ganga.

As a sophomore student at Stevenson High School, I founded Rejuvenate Ganga, an initiative dedicated to restoring and preserving the sacred Ganga River. To manage it beyond any boundaries, I have developed an application to bring together the NGOs, Donators and Volunteers on one common digital platform to enable a seamless and collaborative experience towards achieving one common goal to rejuvenate the holy river “The Ganges”. We must take action, join me in my journey to help clean Ganga.

    This initiative of mine aims at enabling and enhancing collaboration between key stakeholders (Donators, Not-for-Profit Organizations and Volunteers) to achieve:

  • Enhanced integration and collaboration, reducing gaps between planners & executors
  • Agile planning, forecasting and execution
  • Raising, allocating & tracking funds through various sources
  • Planning & conducting last mile campaigns and roadshows
  • Bringing awareness among the masses
  • Clean-up drives
  • River bank stabilization
  • Tree plantation
